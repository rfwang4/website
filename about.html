About Me

I'm a Vancouver-based transportation planner, with experience in transit service planning, co-planning with Indigenous communities, transportation demand management, and active transportation policy.

I grew up in Montreal (with a short stint in Beijing!), so I speak French fluently and Mandarin at an intermediate level. I hold a geography degree from McGill University and a master's degree in community planning from the University of British Columbia.

My interest in urbanism extends to my personal life. I volunteer with Vision Zero Vancouver, a non-profit advocacy group that promotes road safety through better design and effective traffic enforcement. I am also involved in housing advocacy, starting from the premise that housing scarcity is a self-inflicted choice that is directly downstream of restrictive zoning. I sometimes contribute to a blog that I started with friends, Better Columbia.

In my spare time, you'll find me exploring the region by bike, joining a pub trivia night, discovering new indie music (currently obsessed with the Kiwi rock band The Beths!), or at the boxing gym.

The best way to reach me is by email, at ruofan_wang[at]outlook.com.
